Crossover Cables

Eth interfaces detect cable type, if straight through or crossover cable. Long Eth cables have additional noise picked up, data may become distorted. Crossover cable is marked, the word crossover while a straight-through cable is not. Fiber optic cable transmits data over longer distances than copper Eth cable


Copper & Fiber Optic Cable
Copper cables use pulses of electricity to carry signals across a wire. Electric pulse takes 1 & 0. Electric pulse -> 1, Pulse Absence -> 0. 

Fiber jumpers and connector types. || Fiber optic cable uses pulses of light.
Same principle used for fiber optics, instead of electrical pulses, pulses of light.

Glass -> Transports light. Therefore, fiber optic cables use a glass tube in their center to transport the light pulses generated by lasers. The light pulses travel down the glass tube by reflecting off of the sides. They can carry signals for 60 miles before they need regenerated. Smaller the core the farther the light pulse goes before being regenerated.

Single Mode Fiber has a much smaller core than Multi mode Fiber. Smaller core keeps the reflections tighter allowing the optical signal to travel farther.

Fiber Core Size:

  • Multi mode: 50um - 62.5um
  • Single: 8 – 9um

Single Mode fiber for long distances/higher bandwidth & uses a laser as its light source.
Multimode Fiber for short distances/less bandwidth & uses an LED as its light source.

Types of Network Cables
TP = twisted pair, U = Unshielded, C = Coaxial, S = Shielded

  • TP: Copper wires twisted together. Twisting reduces electromagnetic interference(EMI) & crosstalk.
    • UTP: Used in Eth Net. Inexpensive, easy to install, in different categories(Cat). Cat5, Cat6,...,.
    • S/TP: Additional shielding to prevent crosstalk & EMI. Used in high-end networks for better protection
  • C: Cooper Wire surrounded by a braided shield and insulation. Inner conductor carries data signal, while the braided shield protects against EMI.
  • Fiber Optic: Uses light signals to transmit data between devices. Glass or plastic fibers surrounded by jackets. Advantages over copper, + transmission data speed, + bandwidth, IMMUNITY to EMI. Used in long-distance networks.

Cabling Types

  • Point-to-Point: Asset to asset connection directly with a cable. Difficult to manage in large networks.
  • Star Cabling: Asset connected to a central device(hub, switch)/ Easy to manage, easy expansion.
  • Bus Cabling: Asset connected to a single cable, bus, runs along the network. Net failures if cable damaged.

Routers
Forwards data packets between computer networks. Routers are connected to two or + data lines. When a data packet comes in onto one of the lines, the router reads the the network address in the packet to determine the destination. Then, using info in its routing table or routing policy, it directs the packet to its destination.
ISP provides internet → Connect to Modem → Connect Modem to Router → Router shares internet with all your devices.

Routers Functions

  • Packet Forwarding: used between networks
  • Path Selection: Choose a path for the packet 
  • Network Address Translation: devices on priv.
    • network -> transfer data -> public internet
  • Security: Firewalls

Network Switch
Connects multiple devices on a computer network. Used in Eth networks & have routing functionality to process data (layer3). Forwards data only to assets that need to.

  • Multiple ports: Lot of female network connectors

  • VLAN Support Allows network admins to group assets based on common attribs. & network policies

Firewalls
Designed to protect a network by controlling access to and from. Can be hardware, software or both. Inspects all incoming and outgoing traffic to and from the network. It does this by comparing each packet of data against a set of defined rules. If packet data meets the criteria by the rules it can pass by the firewall otherwise not.

  • Packet Filtering Simplest type, analyze each packet that passes through the network. Comparing it to the firewall table rules.
  • Stateful Inspection Analyze individual packets, tracks the connection state between the sender and receiver. If incoming traffic is an existing connection or a new connection request.
  • Application-Level Firewalls Analyzes and blocks apps or protocols. More sophisticated than packet filtering and stateful inspection.

Phone Systems

VOIP Phone System: Allows voice communication over the internet instead of phone lines using IP based networks to transmit voice data in digital packets. Works as long access to internet. Cost savings compared to traditional phone systems for enterprises.
Voice Signal Conversion: Voice signal from callers is converted in digital data packets(codec coder-decoder). Codec compresses the amount of bandwidth(38kb->7kb).
Packetization: Digital data packets are packetized, each packet has voice data, along the source and destination IP.
Transmission: Packets transmitted over an internet, using protocols: TCP/IP, SIP (Session Initiation), and RTP (Real-time Transport).
Reception and Reassembly: Packets received by destination, reassembling them in the correct order. Voice data is extracted and converted back into analog signals.
Voice Signal Output: Reassembled voice is outputted to the receivers, where it is heard.

Advantages VOIP over Analog Phone Systems
Cost savings: international calls. Easy to add new lines, enhances features call forwarding, conferencing, etc.
Disadvantages VOIP over Analog Phone Systems
High-speed internet dependency, can experience Quality of Service (issues, latency, jitter, packet loss). They are vulnerable to hacking, eavesdropping and DoS attacks. Encryption and firewall required. Power dependency

Analog Phone Systems
Traditional system; Analog technology to transmit voice signals over copper wires. Known as Plain Old Telephone Service or Public Switched Telephone Network. Voice signals are transmitted as electrical signals over copper wires. Limited features compared to VOIP. Adding new phone lines can be time-consuming and more cost.

User speaks into the phone, the analog signals are converted into electrical signals, which are transmitted over the phone line to the local exchange. The local exchange then routes the call to the destination using a network of switches and copper wires.

T1 RJ45 Crossover 
T1 circuits are used for voice and data communication. T1C uses RJ45 connectors. However, T1C do not use standard Eth pinouts. To connect two T1 devices together, a T1 RJ45 crossover cable is needed.

This cable crosses over the transmit (Tx) pin on one end of the cable to the receive (Rx) pin on the other end. The Tx and Rx pins are not in the same position on both ends of the cable. This crossover ensures that the transmit signal from one device is correctly received by the other device. Standard Ethernet cable wouldn’t recognize T1 devices with each other.
T1 RJ45 crossover cable required for two T1 devices. If a T1 device needs to be connected to an Eth network, a different type of cable and pinout is required.T1RJ45 crossover cable only needs two pairs
